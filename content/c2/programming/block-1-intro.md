This is the block affiliate athletes skip without knowing it. Three Zone 2 cardio sessions a week, no metcons, no intervals, no leaderboard. If you come from a class culture, this block will feel almost insultingly easy, and that feeling is the most useful diagnostic in the program. The redlined engine you built in classes is impressively powerful and surprisingly shallow; this slow, patient work is what fills it in. The aerobic base adapts on a longer schedule than your competitive instinct wants to allow.

The strength sessions groove the barbell lifts and the power clean at RPE 6-7 and moderate reps, with two-to-three reps left in reserve. Spend this block on bar speed and clean positions, not loads. The power clean in particular is a skill long before it is a strength lift; make the catch fast and confident now, so that in Block 2 you can load it without thinking. By the end of week three, the highest-volume week of the block, you should feel like you could add weight to everything and like you have barely been tested. Good. That is the base being laid.

Then week four. Cut volume by roughly forty percent. In a room that never rests, taking a real deload is the most countercultural thing you will do. Do it anyway. The adaptation happens here.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Easy days stay easy. Hard days stay measured. There are no hard days in Block 1, only lifts to groove and a base to build.
