Block 2 introduces real work above Zone 2 for the first time. Day 4 becomes a tempo session on the rower or air bike: roughly comfortably hard, the pace where you can speak in short phrases but not full sentences. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2 the entire block. The 80/20 ratio holds, even here, even in this room.

Under the bar, the reps drop and the load climbs. The main lifts move to fives and sixes, the power clean sharpens toward heavier, faster singles and doubles, and the front squat and barbell row add volume. The long Day 5 ruck stretches past two hours and the pack gets heavier. By the end of week 7, the peak of Block 2, the lifts that were technique work in week one are moving real weight.

Week 8 is the second deload. Same protocol as week 4: cut sets, drop the load, shorten the cardio, lighten the ruck, sleep more. Block 3 will demand the recovery.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> The long ruck stays easy. The hard work happens under the bar and on Day 4. Do not invert this, no matter what the class on the other side of the room is doing.
