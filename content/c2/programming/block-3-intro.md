Block 3 puts the engine to work. Day 4 becomes a true high-intensity session: short, hard intervals on the air bike or rower, sled pushes, and the occasional measured metcon at 85-90% effort. This is the one place the program lets the room's culture off the leash, and now you can spend that intensity on a base deep enough to support it. This is the only hard session of the week. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2. Strength holds heavy at low reps, with weighted pull-ups, weighted dips, and sled work anchoring the effort.

The long Day 5 ruck approaches its full duration and load: two and a half hours and up to forty-five pounds for Path B by week 11. This is selection-grade work, and it is where the body becomes durable, not just fit. By the end of week 11, you will have what most fit affiliate athletes never build: a deep aerobic base under a strong, powerful body.

Week 12 is the final deload. Take it. After week 12, run the assessment protocols on the next page. They will tell you what twelve weeks of patience, in a room built for impatience, actually built.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Two easy days. One hard day. The long ruck stays easy. The base is what lets the hard day be hard.
