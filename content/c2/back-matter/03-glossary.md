entries:
  - term: "Block periodization"
    definition: "A training structure of three-week build phases followed by one-week deloads. Used by elite strength and endurance programs because tissues adapt on a longer schedule than effort does."
  - term: "Deload"
    definition: "A week of roughly forty percent reduced volume. The point is recovery and consolidation, not laziness. In a room built on intensity, the deload is the most countercultural and most important week of all."
  - term: "RPE"
    definition: "Rate of perceived exertion, on a 1-10 scale. RPE 6-7 means could do two-to-three more clean reps. RPE 8 means could do one more. RPE 9-10 means absolute limit. Most C2 strength work lives at RPE 6-8; the power clean stays fast, not grinding."
  - term: "Zone 2"
    definition: "Low-intensity aerobic training, roughly 60-70% of max heart rate. Gated by the talk test (can speak in full sentences) and nasal breathing. The base affiliate programming most often skips; here it is half the program."
  - term: "Tempo / threshold"
    definition: "Roughly 80-85% effort, comfortably hard. Speak in short phrases, not full sentences. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 2, on the rower or air bike."
  - term: "Intervals"
    definition: "High-intensity work above threshold, with rest between segments. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 3, with sled work and the occasional measured metcon. Done correctly, you cannot speak between breaths during the work segment."
  - term: "Power clean"
    definition: "Pulling a barbell explosively from the floor and receiving it in a partial front-rack squat. Trains power, the ability to produce force quickly, which declines faster than strength with age. Tested for bar speed and a clean catch, never a grind."
  - term: "GHD"
    definition: "The glute-ham developer: a station for back extensions, hip extensions, and sit-ups. Builds the strongest posterior-chain and trunk work in the gym. Scale the range carefully on first exposure; it is easy to overdo."
  - term: "Sled work"
    definition: "Pushing and dragging a weighted sled. All-concentric, so it delivers hard conditioning and leg drive with almost no soreness, which makes it ideal for joint-friendly intensity and recovery between heavy days."
  - term: "Ruck"
    definition: "Walking under load, with a weight vest or a loaded pack, outdoors or on a steep treadmill incline. The Day 5 keystone, and the one session that leaves the gym. Pace stays in Zone 2; the weight provides the stimulus."
  - term: "Path A / Path B"
    definition: "The two stratified tracks of the program. Path A keeps the olympic lifts light and technical for those newer to this room. Path B uses the full barbell and olympic lifts heavy for experienced athletes. Same 12 weeks; different starting points."
  - term: "Keystone session"
    definition: "The long Day 5 ruck. The single most important session of the week. If you do only one session this week, do this one, and do not race it."
  - term: "The tier system"
    definition: "The Stead equipment ladder. The home path runs Tier 0 (bodyweight) through Tier 3 (full home garage). The commercial-gym alternatives are C1 (basic big-box gym) and C2 (serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate). C2 is the top of the ladder, the equal of Tier 3 with more tools and more company."
