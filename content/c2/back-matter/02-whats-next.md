## Stay here, for years

C2 is a destination, not a waypoint. A serious gym has everything you will ever need to build and defend a full-spectrum body for the rest of your life. Run the program again with heavier numbers, rotate the conditioning, vary the lifts. The squat, the deadlift, the clean, and the ruck have a runway measured in decades. The athletes who last are not the ones who chased the hardest metcon at thirty; they are the ones still training, strong and unbroken, at sixty.

## Specialize toward a goal

If you have a specific target, this base is the foundation to build it on. Toward special operations selection, the ruck and the engine become the centerpiece of a more focused preparation. Toward a strength sport or a barbell total, the lifts narrow and the conditioning maintains. Toward a competitive season in your sport, the qualities you built here become the off-season base that the in-season sharpens. Specialization is a branch off this trunk, not a replacement for it.

## The whole ladder, in one body

The Stead ladder runs Tier 0 through Tier 3 at home, and C1 and C2 in the gym, and every rung builds the same body by a different road: useful at 50, durable at 65, still moving on your own at 85. You have walked the arc to its top. The equipment was never the point. The point was the person it built, and the forty years that person gets to keep.
