## Strength assessments

Take a full rest day before testing. Work up in small jumps to a strong, honest top set and stop the moment form degrades. The power clean is tested for clean technique and bar speed, not a grinding max; if the catch falls apart, the number does not count. The squat and deadlift are the headline strength figures, expressed relative to bodyweight because that ratio is what carries over to moving yourself and a load through the world.

## Aerobic assessments

Run these on separate days from the strength tests and from each other. The 2k row is the one most affiliate athletes already know; the difference after twelve weeks of base work is that you will hold a stronger pace with a heart rate that recovers far faster. The ruck standards are the ones that matter most for what the program is ultimately for.

> **IF YOU HIT YOUR PATH'S STANDARDS**
> You are at the top of the Stead ladder, with a base most fit athletes in this room never build. From here, the road forks: specialize toward a sport or toward selection, where this base becomes the foundation for specific preparation, or simply maintain it, deep and durable, for forty more years. There is no higher tier. There is only the rest of your training life.

> **IF YOU DIDN'T HIT THEM YET**
> Run Block 3 again, or run the whole program again, adding small amounts to the lifts you fell short on and guarding the easy days even harder. Pick the two assessments that lagged and concentrate there. Most people who repeat a block hit standards the second time. The Stead trains people who plan to keep training for forty years; one extra month is not a setback, it's a calibration.
