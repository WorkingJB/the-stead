## Path A · The on-ramp

For people arriving from the home path or from C1, comfortable under a bar but newer to olympic lifting and the intensity of a serious room. Path A keeps the power clean light and technical, uses moderate barbell loads at higher reps, and scales the dips and pull-ups, but follows the same block structure and arrives at a real, well-rounded base by week twelve. If the power clean is a skill you are still learning, this is your path; owning it cleanly is one of its explicit goals.

You're on Path A if:

- You can back squat your bodyweight for reps and deadlift comfortably
- The power clean and front squat are newer or rusty patterns for you
- You can do five or more strict pull-ups but limited weighted work
- You are arriving from Tier 3, C1, or a layoff and want to learn this room's tools
- You want the affiliate's toys without the affiliate's redline

## Path B · The build

For experienced athletes who own the barbell lifts and the power clean and want to be deliberate about building toward the SOF and longevity intersection. Path B uses heavier loads at lower reps, the full olympic and barbell lifts, weighted pull-ups and dips, sled work, and longer loaded rucks, but holds the same 80/20 discipline and deload schedule that affiliate culture so often skips. Path B at week twelve has a base equal to anything the Stead ladder can build.

You're on Path B if:

- You can back squat roughly 1.5 times your bodyweight and deadlift around twice
- The power clean, front squat, and olympic patterns are grooved and confident
- A 120-minute ruck at 40 lb is comfortable
- You can do ten or more strict pull-ups and have trained weighted work
- You're injury-free and ready for honest heavy volume, and willing to keep easy days easy

> **WHEN IN DOUBT, START ON PATH A**
> The cost of starting too easy is one week of feeling under-challenged before you graduate up. The cost of starting too hard, especially with a barbell moving fast overhead, is an injury that costs months. Path A is not "the worse path." It is the path that respects what your tissues can actually absorb right now. The strongest athletes in the room are the ones who are still in it at fifty.
