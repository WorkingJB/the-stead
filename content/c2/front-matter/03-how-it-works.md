The twelve weeks are divided into three four-week blocks. Each block is three weeks of progressively loaded work, followed by one deload week at roughly forty percent reduced volume. Deload weeks are not optional and they are not rest weeks. They are when the actual adaptation happens. In a room that prizes intensity, the deload is the most countercultural and most important week of all.

## Block 1: The Engine (weeks 1-4)

Pure aerobic base. Three Zone 2 cardio sessions per week, no intervals, no metcons, no threshold work. The strength sessions groove the barbell lifts and the power clean at RPE 6-7 and moderate reps: technique and bar speed before load. If you come from an affiliate, this block will feel almost insultingly easy. Trust it. The base it builds is the one your conditioning has probably been starving.

## Block 2: The Build (weeks 5-8)

Two Zone 2 sessions per week, plus one tempo or threshold session on the engine machines. Strength reps drop and load climbs, the power clean sharpens, the barbell row and front squat add volume. The long Day 5 ruck stretches out and the pack gets heavier. This is where shape changes happen.

## Block 3: Integration (weeks 9-12)

Two Zone 2 sessions, plus one true high-intensity session: short, hard intervals and the occasional measured metcon on Day 4. Strength holds heavy at low reps, with weighted pull-ups, weighted dips, and sled work anchoring the effort. The ruck reaches its full duration and load. By the end of week twelve, you will have a base that supports everything stacked on top of it, instead of the redlined engine most affiliate athletes run on.

## The 80/20 rule

Across the entire program, roughly eighty percent of your cardio minutes will be at low intensity, and twenty percent at moderate-to-high. This is the ratio elite endurance athletes, special operations training programs, and longevity researchers have independently converged on. It is the ratio affiliate programming inverts most often, with constant high-intensity work and almost no true easy volume, which is exactly why so many fit affiliate athletes have a smaller aerobic base than they think.

> **FIVE DAYS ON, TWO DAYS OFF**
> Days one and three are strength. Days two and four are aerobic. Day five is the long ruck. Day six is optional mobility. Day seven is rest. The order can shift to fit your week, but never stack two hard days back to back.
