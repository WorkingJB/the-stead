## Required (in addition to your Tier 1 and 2 kit)

- A barbell and plates: the centerpiece. A standard 45 lb (20 kg) bar plus enough plates to load the squat and deadlift well past your bodyweight. This is what finally gives you continuous, fine-grained, heavy loading
- A squat rack or stands with safety arms: non-negotiable for squatting and pressing heavy alone. The safeties let you train to honest effort without a spotter. Buy the sturdiest you can fit and afford
- A bench: flat at minimum, adjustable if you can. For the bench press and dumbbell work
- A full range of dumbbells, or adjustable dumbbells: to fill every gap the barbell leaves and to keep the joints happy with unilateral work
- A cardio machine: a rower, an air bike, or a treadmill. This is what makes precise Zone 2 and clean interval work possible in any weather, on any schedule

## Strongly recommended

- A complete kettlebell progression: more than the matched pair from Tier 2, so swings, get-ups, and carries can keep climbing
- Collars that actually hold, and chalk: heavy barbell work punishes a sliding plate and a soft grip
- A pull-up bar that takes added load (you have one from Tier 1); a dip belt for weighted pulls and dips

## The honest gap

There isn't one, not really. Tier 3 is the top of the home path. It can prepare you for selection and maintain a full-spectrum profile for the rest of your life. The only thing a facility adds beyond this is breadth and company: specialty bars, cable stacks, sleds, machines that isolate what a free weight cannot, and other people to train alongside. If a real gym is closer or more motivating than your garage, the commercial tiers C1 and C2 run this same arc there. They are not a higher tier. They are the same training in a different room.

> **A NOTE ON THE BARBELL AND HONESTY**
> The bar will let you load more than your technique can safely handle, which is exactly why it is dangerous and exactly why it works. Use the safety arms. Film your heavy sets. Leave reps in reserve. The trainees who get hurt under a barbell are almost always the ones who let ego set the load. Earn every plate.
