## Path A · The on-ramp

For people arriving from Tier 2, newer to the barbell, or returning to heavy loaded lifting after time away. Path A starts with moderate loads and higher reps, builds technique before intensity, and uses dumbbell variations where they are kinder to the joints, but follows the same block structure and arrives at a real barbell base by week twelve. If the back squat and the deadlift are still patterns you are learning, this is your path; owning them under honest load is one of its explicit goals.

You're on Path A if:

- You can back squat your bodyweight for reps, but not yet much above it
- The deadlift and overhead press are still patterns you are refining
- You are arriving fresh from Tier 2 and want to learn the bar before you load it
- You can do five or more strict pullups but limited weighted work
- You're returning from a layoff and rebuilding under the bar

## Path B · The build

For people with a real barbell base who own the squat, deadlift, press, and bench and want to be deliberate about building toward the SOF and longevity intersection. Path B uses heavier loads at lower reps, the full barbell lifts from the start, weighted pullups and carries, and longer loaded rucks, but holds the same 80/20 discipline and deload schedule. Path B at week twelve is a credible candidate for SOF selection prep or indefinite full-spectrum maintenance.

You're on Path B if:

- You can back squat roughly 1.25 times your bodyweight and deadlift 1.5 times or more
- The squat, deadlift, press, and bench are grooved, autoregulated patterns
- A 120-minute ruck at 40 lb is comfortable
- You can do eight or more strict pullups and have trained weighted pulls
- You're injury-free and ready for honest heavy volume

> **WHEN IN DOUBT, START ON PATH A**
> The cost of starting too easy is one week of feeling under-challenged before you graduate up. The cost of starting too hard is a tweaked back under a loaded bar, which can cost you months. Path A is not "the worse path." It is the path that respects what your tissues can actually absorb right now. Under a barbell, this matters more than at any tier below.
