The Stead is a community organized around four practices that adults used to learn from each other and have largely stopped: keeping a body that works, showing up for the place you live in, knowing how to do useful things with your hands, and feeding yourself and the people around you well. We call these the four pillars: fitness, civic, skills, and food.

Underneath them runs a quieter idea, the one that holds the whole thing together: longevity. Not the marketing kind, with its supplements and biohacks and chasing of numbers. The older, plainer kind. You want to be useful at 50, durable at 65, and still moving on your own at 85. You want your strength to outlast your career and your aerobic engine to outlast your knees.

Tier 2 loaded the legs and shoulders past what one bell allowed. Tier 3 removes the last ceiling the home path had. A barbell and a rack give you continuous, fine-grained, heavy loading, the squat and the deadlift that no amount of clever bell work fully replaces. A full range of dumbbells and kettlebells fills every gap between. A cardio machine lets you hold Zone 2 and run intervals with a precision the road never offered. This is the minimum kit that can credibly prepare a person for special operations selection, and the maximum kit most people will ever need.

## What this is

A twelve-week, block-periodized strength and conditioning program built on a barbell and rack, a bench, a full range of dumbbells and kettlebells, and a cardio machine (rower, bike, or treadmill). Stratified into two paths so it scales to you, whether you are arriving fresh from Tier 2 or starting here already strong under a bar.

## What this isn't

A selection-prep block, a six-week shred, or a peaking program. There is no "before" photo and no finisher week. The Stead trains people who plan to keep training for forty years.
