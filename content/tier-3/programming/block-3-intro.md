Block 3 puts the engine to work. Day 4 becomes a true high-intensity interval session on the machine: four-by-four protocols on the rower, hard air-bike intervals, hill repeats on the treadmill at 85-90% effort. This is the only hard session of the week. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2. Strength holds heavy at low reps, threes and fives near the top of your honest range, with weighted pullups and loaded carries anchoring the work.

The long Day 5 ruck approaches its full duration and load: two and a half hours and up to forty-five pounds for Path B by week 11. This is selection-grade work, and it is where the body becomes durable, not just strong. By the end of week 11, you will have a profile that holds up under real demands.

Week 12 is the final deload. Take it. After week 12, run the assessment protocols on the next page. They will tell you what twelve weeks under the bar actually built.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Two easy days. One hard day. The long ruck stays easy. Heavy bars are earned in the recovery, not stolen from it.
