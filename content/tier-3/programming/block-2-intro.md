Block 2 introduces real work above Zone 2 for the first time. Day 4 becomes a tempo session on the machine: roughly comfortably hard, the pace where you can speak in short phrases but not full sentences. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2 the entire block. The 80/20 ratio holds.

Under the bar, the reps drop and the load climbs. The main lifts move from eights to fives and sixes, where strength is built most efficiently. The barbell row joins the pull day, and the Romanian deadlift sharpens the hinge and protects the heavy deadlift. The long Day 5 ruck stretches past two hours and the pack gets heavier. By the end of week 7, the peak of Block 2, you will be moving loads that would have been a hard single in week one for clean sets of five.

Week 8 is the second deload. Same protocol as week 4: cut sets, drop the load, shorten the cardio, lighten the ruck, sleep more. Block 3 will demand the recovery.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> The long ruck stays easy. The hard work happens under the bar and on Day 4. Do not invert this.
