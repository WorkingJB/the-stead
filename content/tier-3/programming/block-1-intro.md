This is the block most people skip. The block where nothing seems to happen, where the bar feels light and the rucks feel easy. That is the design. The aerobic engine adapts on a longer schedule than your enthusiasm, and the barbell lifts, the squat, the deadlift, the press, the bench, need a slow technical on-ramp before they can absorb heavy load. The connective tissue, in particular, lags the muscle and the will by weeks.

Three Zone 2 cardio sessions per week. No tempo, no intervals, no threshold work. The strength sessions groove the barbell patterns at RPE 6-7 and moderate reps, with two-to-three reps left in reserve on every set. Resist the urge to test your strength in week one; the bar will still be there in week nine, and it will be heavier then because you were patient now. Film your sets. Set the safety arms. By the end of week three, the highest-volume week of the block, you should feel like you could add weight to everything.

Then week four. Cut volume by roughly forty percent. Use the time you save to sleep longer, to ruck easier, and to let your joints catch up to your lifts. The adaptation happens here.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Easy days stay easy. Hard days stay measured. There are no hard days in Block 1, only lifts to groove and a base to build.
