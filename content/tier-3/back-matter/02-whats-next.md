## Stay here, for years

Tier 3 is the destination, not a waypoint. With a barbell, a full range of bells and dumbbells, and a cardio machine, you can train productively for the rest of your life without buying another thing. Cycle the program again with heavier numbers, vary the lifts, rotate the conditioning. The squat and the deadlift have a runway measured in decades. Most people who reach Tier 3 simply live here, running the twelve-week arc on repeat, getting stronger and staying durable.

## The commercial path

If a real gym is closer or more motivating than your garage, the C1 and C2 programs walk this same twelve-week arc using commercial equipment. C1 is the basic big-box gym: machines, dumbbells, a cable stack, cardio equipment, and a limited free-weight area. C2 is a serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate: full barbells, racks, platforms, a full bell and dumbbell range, rowers and bikes, and the company of people training hard. They are not a higher tier than Tier 3. They are the same training in a different room, for the seasons of life when a gym fits better than a garage.

## Toward selection, or toward eighty

A Path B trainee at the Tier 3 standards has two roads open, and they share most of their pavement. One runs toward special operations selection, where this base becomes the foundation for the specific, brutal preparation that job demands. The other runs toward eighty: the same squat, the same ruck, the same engine, maintained and gently progressed for forty more years. The Stead was always built for the second road. That the first one branches off it is the point.
