entries:
  - term: "Block periodization"
    definition: "A training structure of three-week build phases followed by one-week deloads. Used by elite strength and endurance programs because tissues adapt on a longer schedule than effort does."
  - term: "Deload"
    definition: "A week of roughly forty percent reduced volume and load. The point is recovery and consolidation, not laziness. Under a heavy barbell it is also when connective tissue catches up. Skipping deloads is the single most common reason people plateau or break."
  - term: "RPE"
    definition: "Rate of perceived exertion, on a 1-10 scale. RPE 6-7 means could do two-to-three more clean reps. RPE 8 means could do one more. RPE 9-10 means absolute limit. Most Tier 3 barbell work lives at RPE 7-8, never a true max."
  - term: "Reps in reserve (RIR)"
    definition: "How many additional clean reps you could have done at the end of a set. Two RIR is the default Tier 3 target on the main lifts; it keeps heavy training safe and repeatable."
  - term: "Zone 2"
    definition: "Low-intensity aerobic training, roughly 60-70% of max heart rate. Gated by the talk test (can speak in full sentences) and nasal breathing. On a machine, the power or pace that holds that effort. The foundation of every endurance and tactical program ever built."
  - term: "Tempo / threshold"
    definition: "Roughly 80-85% effort, comfortably hard. Speak in short phrases, not full sentences. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 2, on the cardio machine."
  - term: "Intervals"
    definition: "High-intensity work above threshold, with rest between segments. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 3, on the machine (4x4 rower protocol, air-bike intervals, treadmill hills). Done correctly, you cannot speak between breaths during the work segment."
  - term: "The main lifts"
    definition: "The barbell back squat, deadlift, overhead press, and bench press. The four movements that carry the most strength per minute of training time, and the spine of Tier 3."
  - term: "Safety arms / spotter arms"
    definition: "The adjustable catches in a squat rack that hold a failed barbell. Non-negotiable for squatting and pressing heavy without a spotter. Set them every session."
  - term: "Ruck"
    definition: "Walking under load, with a weight vest or a loaded pack. The Day 5 keystone of the program. Pace stays in Zone 2; the weight provides the stimulus. At Tier 3 the loads approach selection grade."
  - term: "Path A / Path B"
    definition: "The two stratified tracks of the program. Path A is the on-ramp for those newer to the barbell or arriving from Tier 2. Path B is the build for those with a real barbell base. Same 12 weeks; different starting loads and reps; same destination."
  - term: "Keystone session"
    definition: "The long Day 5 ruck. The single most important session of the week. If you do only one session this week, do this one."
  - term: "Tier 0 / Tier 1 / etc."
    definition: "The Stead equipment tier system. The home path runs Tier 0 (bodyweight) through Tier 3 (full home garage), each tier adding equipment and capability. Tier 3 is the top of the home path. The commercial-gym alternatives are labeled C1 (basic big-box gym) and C2 (serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate)."
