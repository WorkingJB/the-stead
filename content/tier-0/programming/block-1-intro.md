This is the block most people skip. The block where nothing seems to happen, where the soreness from week one fades into the discipline of a daily walk, where the strength sessions feel almost too easy. That is the design. The aerobic engine adapts on a longer schedule than your enthusiasm, and the connective tissues need a slow on-ramp before they can absorb real load.

Three Zone 2 cardio sessions per week. No tempo, no intervals, no threshold work. The strength sessions teach the patterns at RPE 6-7, with two-to-three reps left in reserve on every set. By the end of week three, the highest-volume week of the block, you should feel like you could do another set on everything you did.

Then week four. Cut volume by roughly forty percent. Use the time you save to sleep longer, to walk easier, and to feel what an actual recovery week does to a body that has been training. The adaptation happens here.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Easy days stay easy. Hard days stay measured. There are no hard days in Block 1.
