Block 2 introduces real work above Zone 2 for the first time. Day 4 becomes a tempo session: roughly comfortably hard, the pace where you can speak in short phrases but not full sentences. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2 the entire block. The 80/20 ratio holds.

Strength volume climbs. Movement variations get slightly harder. The pushup might move from incline to standard; the squat from box to free; the row to a steeper lean. Long Day 5 stretches past an hour and starts carrying a light pack. By the end of week 7, the peak of Block 2, you will feel like a different athlete than the one who started.

Week 8 is the second deload. Same protocol as week 4: cut sets, shorten cardio, sleep more. Block 3 will demand the recovery.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> The long day stays easy. The hard work happens on Day 4. Do not invert this.
