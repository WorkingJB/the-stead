entries:
  - term: "Block periodization"
    definition: "A training structure of three-week build phases followed by one-week deloads. Used by elite strength and endurance programs because tissues adapt on a longer schedule than effort does."
  - term: "Deload"
    definition: "A week of roughly forty percent reduced volume. The point is recovery and consolidation, not laziness. Skipping deloads is the single most common reason people plateau or break."
  - term: "RPE"
    definition: "Rate of perceived exertion, on a 1-10 scale. RPE 6-7 means could do two-to-three more clean reps. RPE 8 means could do one more. RPE 9-10 means absolute limit. Most Tier 0 work lives at RPE 6-8."
  - term: "Zone 2"
    definition: "Low-intensity aerobic training, roughly 60-70% of max heart rate. Gated by the talk test (can speak in full sentences) and nasal breathing. The foundation of every endurance and tactical program ever built."
  - term: "Tempo / threshold"
    definition: "Roughly 80-85% effort, comfortably hard. Speak in short phrases, not full sentences. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 2."
  - term: "Intervals"
    definition: "High-intensity work above threshold, with rest between segments. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 3. Done correctly, you cannot speak between breaths during the work segment."
  - term: "Reps in reserve (RIR)"
    definition: "How many additional clean reps you could have done at the end of a set. Two RIR is the default Tier 0 target."
  - term: "Path A / Path B"
    definition: "The two stratified tracks of the program. Path A is the on-ramp for returning or beginning trainees. Path B is the build for those with a base. Same 12 weeks; different starting points; same Tier 1 finish line."
  - term: "Keystone session"
    definition: "The long Day 5 walk. The single most important session of the week. If you do only one session this week, do this one."
  - term: "Tier 0 / Tier 1 / etc."
    definition: "The Stead equipment tier system. Tier 0 is bodyweight only. Each tier above adds equipment and capability, up to Tier 5 (full home garage). Commercial-gym tiers are labeled C1 through C4."
