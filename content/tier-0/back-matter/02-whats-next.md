## Tier 1 - The Standard Home

A doorway pullup bar (closes the pulling gap that Tier 0 could only approximate). A heavy kettlebell, 16-24 kg depending on Path and body size. A weight vest, ideally 20-30 lb. Real training shoes. Total cost: $300-500. With this kit, you have what most operators trained on for the first decade of their career.

The Tier 1 program is twelve weeks, like Tier 0, but the strength sessions become kettlebell-based and the long day becomes a real ruck. Most graduates of Tier 0 spend two to three Tier 1 cycles before considering Tier 2.

## Tier 2 - The Expanded Home

A second kettlebell (or the first if you skipped Tier 1's heavy bell), gymnastics rings, a sandbag, a jump rope, a plyo box. The complete bodyweight + ballistic toolkit. From Tier 2 onward, the limit becomes attention, not equipment.

## Tier 3 - The Real Garage

A cardio machine (rower, bike, or treadmill). A range of dumbbells. A full kettlebell progression. The minimum equipment to credibly prepare for SOF selection or to maintain a full-spectrum fitness profile indefinitely.

## The commercial path

If a real gym is closer to you than a real garage, the C1 and C2 programs walk the same twelve-week arc using commercial equipment. C1 is the basic big-box gym; C2 is a serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate.
