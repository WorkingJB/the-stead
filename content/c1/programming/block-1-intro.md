This is the block most people skip. The block where nothing seems to happen, where the weights feel light and the rucks feel easy. That is the design. The aerobic engine adapts on a longer schedule than your enthusiasm, and the movements, even the machine ones, need a few weeks of grooving before they can absorb real load.

Three Zone 2 cardio sessions per week. No tempo, no intervals, no threshold work. The strength sessions learn the machines and the dumbbell patterns at RPE 6-7, with two-to-three reps left in reserve on every set. Spend this block setting the seats and pins where they belong, learning how each movement should feel, and dialing the assisted pull-up to where honest full-range reps are possible. Do not chase the stack in week one. By the end of week three, the highest-volume week of the block, you should feel like you could add weight to everything.

Then week four. Cut volume by roughly forty percent. Use the time you save to sleep longer, to ruck easier, and to feel what an actual recovery week does to a body that has been training. The adaptation happens here.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Easy days stay easy. Hard days stay measured. There are no hard days in Block 1, only patterns to learn well.
