Block 3 puts the engine to work. Day 4 becomes a true high-intensity interval session on a cardio machine: hard rower or bike intervals, treadmill hill repeats at 85-90% effort. This is the only hard session of the week. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2. Strength holds at Block 2 volume with the heaviest honest loads of the program, and the pull-up work takes off the last of the assistance, reaching for your first unassisted reps.

The long Day 5 ruck approaches its full duration and load: over two hours and up to thirty-five pounds for Path B by week 11. This is where the body becomes durable, not just strong. By the end of week 11, you will be a credible candidate for C2 or for the home garage if you want to load a barbell.

Week 12 is the final deload. Take it. After week 12, run the assessment protocols on the next page. They will tell you what twelve weeks of a real plan, on a basic gym floor, actually built.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Two easy days. One hard day. The long ruck stays easy.
