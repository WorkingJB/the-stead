## What you will use

- Selectorized machines: a chest press, a shoulder press, a lat pulldown, a seated row, a leg press, a leg curl, and a leg extension cover every basic pattern with a guided, solo-safe path of motion
- A full dumbbell range: for presses, rows, squats, lunges, Romanian deadlifts, and carries. The most versatile free weights on the floor, and the bridge from the machines to the barbell
- A cable stack or functional trainer: for rows, pulldowns, pull-throughs, face pulls, and the anti-rotation core work that machines cannot train
- An assisted pull-up and dip station: the fastest honest road to your first strict pull-up, and a Path A staple
- The cardio floor: treadmills, bikes, ellipticals, and usually a rower or two, for precise Zone 2 and the Day 4 conditioning

## For the ruck

- A weight vest, or a backpack loaded with soft, stable weight: the Day 5 ruck is done outdoors when you can, or on a treadmill set to a steep incline when you cannot. The incline walk under a vest is a genuinely good substitute, and it is always available

## The honest gap

A basic gym is built for general fitness, not for heavy barbell strength. The squat racks may be few, crowded, or replaced by a Smith machine; there is rarely a platform for heavy deadlifts and rarely bumper plates to drop them. C1 works around this with the leg press, heavy dumbbell and goblet squats, dumbbell Romanian deadlifts, and hip thrusts, which build the legs and posterior chain very well within these limits. When you want to load the squat and the deadlift with a free barbell, past what dumbbells and machines allow, that is the signal for C2: a serious strength gym with racks and platforms.

> **A NOTE ON MAKING THE FLOOR YOURS**
> Everyone at the gym is thinking about their own set, not yours. Take the machine. Ask for the dumbbells. Work in on the rack. The single biggest thing standing between most members and real progress is not the equipment; it is the quiet belief that the free-weight floor belongs to someone else. It belongs to whoever shows up with a plan. That is now you.
