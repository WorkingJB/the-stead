The twelve weeks are divided into three four-week blocks. Each block is three weeks of progressively loaded work, followed by one deload week at roughly forty percent reduced volume. Deload weeks are not optional and they are not rest weeks. They are when the actual adaptation happens.

## Block 1: The Engine (weeks 1-4)

Pure aerobic base. Three Zone 2 cardio sessions per week, no intervals, no threshold work. The strength sessions learn the machines and dumbbell patterns at RPE 6-7: where the seat pins go, how each movement should feel, how to take a set close to honest effort without grinding. We are grooving patterns, not chasing the stack. This block is the one most people skip; it is also the one that makes the next eight weeks possible.

## Block 2: The Build (weeks 5-8)

Two Zone 2 sessions per week, plus one tempo or threshold session on a cardio machine. Strength volume and load climb: the dumbbells get heavier, the machine pins drop lower, the cable and free-weight work adds sets. The long Day 5 ruck stretches out and the pack gets heavier. This is where shape changes happen.

## Block 3: Integration (weeks 9-12)

Two Zone 2 sessions, plus one true high-intensity interval session on a cardio machine. Strength holds at Block 2 volume with the heaviest honest loads of the program, and the assisted pull-up work progresses toward your first unassisted reps. The ruck reaches its full duration and load. By the end of week twelve, you will have a base that supports everything stacked on top of it, instead of the glass cannon most twelve-week programs produce.

## The 80/20 rule

Across the entire program, roughly eighty percent of your cardio minutes will be at low intensity, and twenty percent at moderate-to-high. This is the ratio elite endurance athletes, special operations training programs, and longevity researchers have independently converged on. It is also the ratio that almost every recreational trainee inverts, which is why most recreational trainees plateau.

> **FIVE DAYS ON, TWO DAYS OFF**
> Days one and three are strength. Days two and four are aerobic. Day five is the long ruck. Day six is optional mobility. Day seven is rest. The order can shift to fit your week, but never stack two hard days back to back.
