entries:
  - term: "Block periodization"
    definition: "A training structure of three-week build phases followed by one-week deloads. Used by elite strength and endurance programs because tissues adapt on a longer schedule than effort does."
  - term: "Deload"
    definition: "A week of roughly forty percent reduced volume. The point is recovery and consolidation, not laziness. Skipping deloads is the single most common reason people plateau or break."
  - term: "RPE"
    definition: "Rate of perceived exertion, on a 1-10 scale. RPE 6-7 means could do two-to-three more clean reps. RPE 8 means could do one more. RPE 9-10 means absolute limit. Most C1 strength work lives at RPE 6-8."
  - term: "Zone 2"
    definition: "Low-intensity aerobic training, roughly 60-70% of max heart rate. Gated by the talk test (can speak in full sentences) and nasal breathing. The foundation of every endurance and tactical program ever built. Trust it over the cardio machine's built-in zones."
  - term: "Tempo / threshold"
    definition: "Roughly 80-85% effort, comfortably hard. Speak in short phrases, not full sentences. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 2, on a cardio machine."
  - term: "Intervals"
    definition: "High-intensity work above threshold, with rest between segments. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 3. Done correctly, you cannot speak between breaths during the work segment."
  - term: "Selectorized machine"
    definition: "A weight-stack machine with a guided path of motion, selected by a pin. Lets a trainee at any level take a set close to honest effort safely and alone. The anchor of Path A strength work."
  - term: "Assisted pull-up"
    definition: "A pull-up done on a station that counterweights part of your bodyweight, allowing full-range reps. Reduce the assistance over the weeks to build toward your first unassisted pull-up."
  - term: "Ruck"
    definition: "Walking under load, with a weight vest or a loaded pack, outdoors or on a steep treadmill incline. The Day 5 keystone of the program. Pace stays in Zone 2; the weight provides the stimulus."
  - term: "Path A / Path B"
    definition: "The two stratified tracks of the program. Path A leans on the machines and the assisted pull-up for those new to the gym. Path B leans on the free weights and strict pull-ups for those with a base. Same 12 weeks; different starting points."
  - term: "Keystone session"
    definition: "The long Day 5 ruck. The single most important session of the week. If you do only one session this week, do this one."
  - term: "The tier system"
    definition: "The Stead equipment ladder. The home path runs Tier 0 (bodyweight) through Tier 3 (full home garage). The commercial-gym alternatives are C1 (basic big-box gym) and C2 (serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate). C1 and C2 are not higher than the home tiers; they are the same training in a gym."
