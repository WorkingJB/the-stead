## Strength assessments

Take a full rest day before testing. Each test is one all-out set, full range of motion, controlled tempo. Stop the set the rep before form breaks. The pull-up is the headline number of this program; if you arrived unable to do one, getting your first unassisted rep is a real milestone, and a common one by week twelve.

## Aerobic assessments

Run these on separate days from the strength tests and from each other. They are efforts, not races; hold a pace you could sustain. The ruck standard is the one that matters most for what the program is ultimately for.

> **IF YOU HIT YOUR PATH'S STANDARDS**
> You have proven that a basic gym card builds a real engine and real strength. From here you have three good roads: run the program again with heavier numbers, move to C2 for the barbells and platforms of a serious strength gym, or set up a home garage at Tier 3. The clearest signal to move on is wanting to load the squat and deadlift with a free barbell, past what dumbbells and machines allow.

> **IF YOU DIDN'T HIT THEM YET**
> Run Block 3 again, or run the whole program again with heavier loads. Pick the two assessments you fell short on and concentrate volume there. Most people who repeat a block hit standards the second time. The Stead trains people who plan to keep training for forty years; one extra month is not a setback, it's a calibration.
