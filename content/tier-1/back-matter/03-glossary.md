entries:
  - term: "Block periodization"
    definition: "A training structure of three-week build phases followed by one-week deloads. Used by elite strength and endurance programs because tissues adapt on a longer schedule than effort does."
  - term: "Deload"
    definition: "A week of roughly forty percent reduced volume. The point is recovery and consolidation, not laziness. Skipping deloads is the single most common reason people plateau or break."
  - term: "RPE"
    definition: "Rate of perceived exertion, on a 1-10 scale. RPE 6-7 means could do two-to-three more clean reps. RPE 8 means could do one more. RPE 9-10 means absolute limit. Most Tier 1 strength work lives at RPE 6-8."
  - term: "Zone 2"
    definition: "Low-intensity aerobic training, roughly 60-70% of max heart rate. Gated by the talk test (can speak in full sentences) and nasal breathing. The foundation of every endurance and tactical program ever built. Under a weight vest, your pace must drop to hold the same effort."
  - term: "Tempo / threshold"
    definition: "Roughly 80-85% effort, comfortably hard. Speak in short phrases, not full sentences. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 2."
  - term: "Intervals"
    definition: "High-intensity work above threshold, with rest between segments. Introduced on Day 4 in Block 3. Done correctly, you cannot speak between breaths during the work segment."
  - term: "Reps in reserve (RIR)"
    definition: "How many additional clean reps you could have done at the end of a set. Two RIR is the default Tier 1 target on strength work."
  - term: "The rack / front rack"
    definition: "The kettlebell resting position: bell on the back of the wrist and forearm, forearm vertical, elbow tucked to the ribs. The start of the press and the loaded position of the front-rack squat."
  - term: "Ruck"
    definition: "Walking under load, with a weight vest or a loaded pack. The Day 5 keystone of Tier 1. Pace stays in Zone 2; the weight provides the stimulus."
  - term: "Path A / Path B"
    definition: "The two stratified tracks of the program. Path A is the on-ramp for those new to the kettlebell or arriving from Tier 0. Path B is the build for those with a base who own the pullup. Same 12 weeks; different starting points; same Tier 2 finish line."
  - term: "Keystone session"
    definition: "The long Day 5 ruck. The single most important session of the week. If you do only one session this week, do this one."
  - term: "Tier 0 / Tier 1 / etc."
    definition: "The Stead equipment tier system. The home path runs Tier 0 (bodyweight) through Tier 3 (full home garage), each tier adding equipment and capability. The commercial-gym alternatives are labeled C1 (basic big-box gym) and C2 (serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate)."
