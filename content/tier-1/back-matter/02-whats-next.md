## Tier 2 - The Expanded Home

A second kettlebell (which unlocks double cleans, double front squats, and double presses), gymnastics rings, a sandbag, a jump rope, and a plyo box. The complete bodyweight and ballistic toolkit. The clearest sign you are ready is simple: when one bell no longer challenges your squat and press, you have outgrown the single-bell ceiling. From Tier 2 onward, the limit becomes attention, not equipment.

## Tier 3 - The Real Garage

A cardio machine (rower, bike, or treadmill). A range of dumbbells. A full kettlebell progression. The minimum equipment to credibly prepare for SOF selection or to maintain a full-spectrum fitness profile indefinitely. Most people never need more than this.

## The commercial path

If a real gym is closer to you than a real garage, the C1 and C2 programs walk the same twelve-week arc using commercial equipment. C1 is the basic big-box gym; C2 is a serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate. The tier system runs Tier 0 through Tier 3 for the home path, with C1 and C2 as the commercial alternatives.

## Or stay here

There is no obligation to climb. Tier 1 can be run for years: cycle the bell heavier, the vest heavier, the rucks longer, and you will keep adapting. Two or three Tier 1 cycles before Tier 2 is the common and entirely respectable road.
