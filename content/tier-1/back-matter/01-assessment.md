## Strength assessments

Take a full rest day before testing. Each test is one all-out set, full range of motion, controlled tempo. Stop the set the rep before form breaks. The pullup is the headline number of this tier; if you arrived from Tier 0 unable to do one, getting to three is a real milestone.

## Aerobic assessments

Run these on separate days from the strength tests and from each other. They are efforts, not races; hold a pace you could sustain. The ruck standards are the ones that matter most for what comes next.

> **IF YOU HIT YOUR PATH'S STANDARDS**
> You are ready for Tier 2. The clearest signal is the strength work itself: when one kettlebell no longer challenges your squat and press for the prescribed reps, it is time for a second bell. Tier 2 adds that bell plus rings, a sandbag, a jump rope, and a plyo box.

> **IF YOU DIDN'T HIT THEM YET**
> Run Block 3 again, or run the whole tier again with a heavier bell or vest. Pick the two assessments you fell short on and concentrate volume there. Most people who repeat a block hit standards the second time. The Stead trains people who plan to keep training for forty years; one extra month is not a setback, it's a calibration.
