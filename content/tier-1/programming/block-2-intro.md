Block 2 introduces real work above Zone 2 for the first time. Day 4 becomes a tempo session: roughly comfortably hard, the pace where you can speak in short phrases but not full sentences. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2 the entire block. The 80/20 ratio holds.

Strength volume and load both climb. The single-arm front-rack squat enters as a heavier squat than the goblet allows. The swing progresses toward the single-arm version. Pullups add a pause at the top and more total volume, and the bent-over row joins the pull day. The long Day 5 ruck stretches past ninety minutes and the vest gets heavier. By the end of week 7, the peak of Block 2, you will feel like a different athlete than the one who started.

Week 8 is the second deload. Same protocol as week 4: cut sets, shorten the cardio, lighten the ruck, sleep more. Block 3 will demand the recovery.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> The long ruck stays easy. The hard work happens on Day 4. Do not invert this.
