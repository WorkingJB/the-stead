Block 3 puts the engine to work. Day 4 becomes a true high-intensity interval session: four-by-four protocols, hill repeats, or sustained interval work at 85-90% effort. This is the only hard session of the week. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2. Strength holds at Block 2 volume, with two additions that define the block: weighted pullups with the vest, and the Turkish get-up for Path B.

The long Day 5 ruck approaches its full duration and load: two hours and up to thirty-five pounds for Path B by week 11. This is where the body becomes durable, not just strong. By the end of week 11, you will be a credible candidate for Tier 2.

Week 12 is the final deload. Take it. After week 12, run the assessment protocols on the next page. If you hit Path B standards, and especially if one bell no longer challenges the squat and press, Tier 2 and its second kettlebell are yours.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Two easy days. One hard day. The long ruck stays easy.
