The Stead is a community organized around four practices that adults used to learn from each other and have largely stopped: keeping a body that works, showing up for the place you live in, knowing how to do useful things with your hands, and feeding yourself and the people around you well. We call these the four pillars: fitness, civic, skills, and food.

Underneath them runs a quieter idea, the one that holds the whole thing together: longevity. Not the marketing kind, with its supplements and biohacks and chasing of numbers. The older, plainer kind. You want to be useful at 50, durable at 65, and still moving on your own at 85. You want your strength to outlast your career and your aerobic engine to outlast your knees.

Tier 1 gave you the first real kit: a bar to pull yourself over and a single bell to scale for years. Tier 2 removes the one ceiling that kit had. A second kettlebell unlocks true double-bell strength, the double front squat and double press that finally load the legs and shoulders the way a barbell would. Gymnastics rings turn pushing and pulling into a skill you can scale for life. A sandbag teaches you to move an awkward, shifting load, which is what real life mostly asks of you. A jump rope and a plyo box add the power and footwork the program has not had room for until now.

## What this is

A twelve-week, block-periodized strength and conditioning program built on two kettlebells, gymnastics rings, a sandbag, a jump rope, and a plyo box. Stratified into two paths so it scales to you, whether you are arriving fresh from Tier 1 or starting here with a real double-bell base already built.

## What this isn't

A selection-prep block, a six-week shred, or a peaking program. There is no "before" photo and no finisher week. The Stead trains people who plan to keep training for forty years.
