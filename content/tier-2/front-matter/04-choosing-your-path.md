## Path A · The on-ramp

For people arriving from Tier 1, new to double-bell work and the rings, or returning to loaded training after time away. Path A starts with the goblet squat and lighter bells, ring pushups instead of full dips, and shorter rucks, but follows the same block structure and arrives at the same Tier 3 readiness by week twelve. If the double front squat and the ring dip are new to you, this is your path; owning them cleanly is one of its explicit goals.

You're on Path A if:

- You can do three to five strict pullups but not yet a clean weighted set
- The double clean and double front squat are new or rusty patterns for you
- A full-depth ring dip is not yet on the menu
- You are arriving fresh from Tier 1 and want to learn the new tools first
- You're returning from a layoff and rebuilding loaded capacity

## Path B · The build

For people with a real double-bell base who own the pullup and the ring dip and want to be deliberate about building toward the SOF and longevity intersection. Path B uses the heavier pair of bells, weighted pullups and ring dips, the sandbag from the shoulder, and longer loaded rucks from the start, but holds the same 80/20 discipline and deload schedule. Path B at week twelve is a credible candidate for Tier 3.

You're on Path B if:

- You can do eight or more strict pullups and dip cleanly on the rings
- The double clean, double front squat, and double press are grooved patterns
- A 120-minute ruck at 30 lb is comfortable
- You have completed a full Tier 1 cycle, or its equivalent, with consistent loaded work
- You're injury-free and ready for honest volume

> **WHEN IN DOUBT, START ON PATH A**
> The cost of starting too easy is one week of feeling under-challenged before you graduate up. The cost of starting too hard is an overuse injury, a tweaked shoulder on the rings, or quitting in week three. Path A is not "the worse path." It is the path that respects what your tissues can actually absorb right now.
