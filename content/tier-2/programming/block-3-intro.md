Block 3 puts the engine to work. Day 4 becomes a true high-intensity interval session: four-by-four protocols, hill repeats, box jumps, and jump-rope intervals at 85-90% effort. This is the only hard session of the week. Days 2 and 5 stay Zone 2. Strength holds at Block 2 volume, with three additions that define the block: weighted pullups, weighted ring dips, and the Turkish get-up anchoring Path B's pull day.

The long Day 5 ruck approaches its full duration and load: two and a half hours and up to forty pounds for Path B by week 11. This is where the body becomes durable, not just strong. By the end of week 11, you will be a credible candidate for Tier 3.

Week 12 is the final deload. Take it. After week 12, run the assessment protocols on the next page. If you hit Path B standards, and especially if the double front squat and the sandbag no longer challenge your legs, Tier 3 and its rower, dumbbells, and barbell are yours.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Two easy days. One hard day. The long ruck stays easy.
