This is the block most people skip. The block where nothing seems to happen, where the strength sessions feel almost too easy and the rucks feel almost too light. That is the design. The aerobic engine adapts on a longer schedule than your enthusiasm, and the new patterns, the double clean, the ring dip, the sandbag off the floor, need a slow on-ramp before they can absorb real load.

Three Zone 2 cardio sessions per week. No tempo, no intervals, no threshold work. The strength sessions groove the double-bell and ring patterns at RPE 6-7, with two-to-three reps left in reserve on every set. The double clean to the rack is a skill before it is a strength lift; spend this block making it automatic, so that in Block 2 you can squat and press from a rack you trust. If the rings feel shaky, that is the point, and it settles fast. By the end of week three, the highest-volume week of the block, you should feel like you could do another set on everything.

Then week four. Cut volume by roughly forty percent. Use the time you save to sleep longer, to ruck easier, and to feel what an actual recovery week does to a body that has been training. The adaptation happens here.

> **THE RULE OF THE BLOCK**
> Easy days stay easy. Hard days stay measured. There are no hard days in Block 1, only patterns to learn well.
