## Strength assessments

Take a full rest day before testing. Each test is one all-out set, full range of motion, controlled tempo. Stop the set the rep before form breaks. The double front squat and the ring dip are the headline numbers of this tier; they tell you whether the new tools have become real strength or are still just skills.

## Aerobic assessments

Run these on separate days from the strength tests and from each other. They are efforts, not races; hold a pace you could sustain. The ruck standards are the ones that matter most for what comes next.

> **IF YOU HIT YOUR PATH'S STANDARDS**
> You are ready for Tier 3. The clearest signal is the strength work itself: when the double front squat and the sandbag no longer challenge your legs for the prescribed reps, the home kit has given what it can. Tier 3 adds a cardio machine, a full dumbbell range, a complete kettlebell progression, and a barbell.

> **IF YOU DIDN'T HIT THEM YET**
> Run Block 3 again, or run the whole tier again with heavier bells, a heavier sandbag, or weighted ring work. Pick the two assessments you fell short on and concentrate volume there. Most people who repeat a block hit standards the second time. The Stead trains people who plan to keep training for forty years; one extra month is not a setback, it's a calibration.
