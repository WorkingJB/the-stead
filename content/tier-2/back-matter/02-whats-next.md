## Tier 3 - The Real Garage

A cardio machine (rower, bike, or treadmill). A full range of dumbbells. A complete kettlebell progression. And, for most people, a barbell. The minimum equipment to credibly prepare for SOF selection or to maintain a full-spectrum fitness profile indefinitely. The clearest sign you are ready is simple: when the double front squat and the sandbag no longer challenge your legs, you have reached the ceiling of the awkward-load kit and want continuous, heavy, fine-grained loading. Most people never need more than Tier 3.

## The commercial path

If a real gym is closer to you than a real garage, the C1 and C2 programs walk the same twelve-week arc using commercial equipment. C1 is the basic big-box gym; C2 is a serious strength gym or CrossFit affiliate. The tier system runs Tier 0 through Tier 3 for the home path, with C1 and C2 as the commercial alternatives.

## Or stay here

There is no obligation to climb. Tier 2 can be run for years: cycle the bells heavier, the sandbag heavier, the ring work harder, the rucks longer, and you will keep adapting. Two or three Tier 2 cycles before Tier 3 is the common and entirely respectable road. The double front squat and the weighted ring dip have a long runway before they stop paying out.
