# Style Rules

Hard rules for every tier and every page. These are not suggestions. Apply them retroactively to any inherited content.

## Punctuation

- **Never use em dashes (the long dash).** Use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses instead. This applies to body prose, headings, callouts, glossary entries, everything. If a sentence wants an em dash, restructure it.
- En dashes are fine for numeric ranges (`8-12`, `45-60 min`, `RPE 6-7`). A plain hyphen is also acceptable for ranges; be consistent within a document.
- Use the multiplication sign `×` (not the letter `x`) in prescriptions: `3 × 8`, not `3 x 8`.

## Prescription format

- **Colon between the movement and the prescription.** `Wall pushup: 3 × 8`. Never a period (`Wall pushup. 3 × 8`) and never an em dash.
- Sets first, then reps or duration: `3 × 8-10`, `4 × 60 sec`, `2 × 6 min`.
- Per-side movements say "each side" or "ea side": `Dead bug: 3 × 6 each side`.
- Holds use seconds explicitly: `Plank: 3 × 45 sec` or `Side plank: 3 × 30s each side`.
- Cardio is duration first, then qualifier: `30 min brisk walking, flat to gently rolling terrain`.

## Tone in chat (when working on this project)

- Concise back-and-forth. Ask clarifying questions when there are real choices.
- Minimal formatting. No bullet-list when declining or explaining a small thing.

## Numbers and units

- Spell out numbers in flowing prose where it reads better ("Three blocks, twelve weeks", "forty percent"), but use digits in prescriptions, ranges, and data (`3 × 8`, `80/20`, `RPE 6-7`, `20 lb`).
- Pounds as `lb` (no period), minutes as `min`, seconds as `sec` or `s` in tight tables.
- Percentages spelled out in prose ("roughly eighty percent"), digits in data.

## Capitalization

- **Eyebrows / labels / running heads:** UPPERCASE with wide letter-spacing (a typographic treatment, authored in normal case in source where practical, e.g. front-matter `eyebrow:`).
- **Proper nouns of the system:** Zone 2, Path A, Path B, Block 1, Tier 0, The Stead, The Engine, The Build, Integration.
- Movement names in the library use Title Case (`Doorway Towel Row`, `Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift`). Inside prescriptions they may be sentence case to read naturally.

## Voice reminders (full detail in voice-guide.md)

- Second person, direct, plain. "You want to be useful at 50."
- Earned aphorism at the close of a section ("Train the squat; outlive the chair.").
- No hype, no "before" photos, no finisher weeks. The long floor over the high ceiling.
